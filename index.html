The Warner Brothers

Four Brothers. One Empire.

Sons of a Polish immigrant cobbler — Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner began with a projector and $150, and built the world's greatest entertainment studio.

HW
Harry Warner
President · 1923–1956
b. 1881, Krasnosielc, Poland

The eldest brother and company president, Harry ran New York headquarters overseeing finance and distribution. He championed the studio's founding motto: "Pictures that educate, enlighten and entertain." His business acumen funded Sam's technological gambles and built the studio's financial foundation.

SW
Sam Warner
Technology Visionary
b. 1887, Krasnosielc, Poland

Sam championed Vitaphone synchronized-sound technology, producing The Jazz Singer (1927) — the first major talking picture. He convinced his skeptical brothers to bet the studio on it, and won. Heartbreakingly, Sam died of a cerebral hemorrhage the night before its premiere, never witnessing the revolution he created.

JW
Jack L. Warner
Head of Production · 1923–1967
b. 1892, London, Ontario, Canada

The Hollywood showman, Jack signed Humphrey Bogart, Bette Davis, Errol Flynn, and James Cagney. He led the studio through its golden era, introduced television production in 1955 with Cheyenne for ABC, and held creative control until the studio's 1967 sale to Seven Arts Productions.

Albert (Abe) Warner (b. 1884, Krasnosielc, Poland) — Co-Founder & Treasurer — oversaw all distribution and sales from New York City.

Timeline

102 Years of History

Select a year to explore verified milestones from over a century of cinematic innovation.

1903

The brothers open the Cascade — their first movie theater — in New Castle, Pennsylvania, with $150 and a single projector showing The Great Train Robbery.

1923

Warner Bros. Pictures, Inc. is officially incorporated on April 4, 1923, in Burbank, California, by Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner.

1927

The Jazz Singer — the first feature-length talking picture — premieres. Sam Warner dies the night before, never witnessing the revolution he created.

1942

Casablanca premieres. It wins Best Picture at the Academy Awards and becomes one of the greatest films ever made.

1955

Warner Bros. Television launches Cheyenne for ABC — television's first hour-long Western — pivoting the studio into the TV era.

1990

Warner Communications merges with Time Inc. forming Time Warner — the world's largest media company at the time.

2001

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone launches the highest-grossing film series in history (not adjusted for inflation).

2022

Warner Bros. Discovery formed April 8 after the $43B AT&T WarnerMedia–Discovery merger. David Zaslav becomes President & CEO.

2023

Warner Bros. celebrates 100 years. Barbie (2023) directed by Greta Gerwig crosses $1.4 billion worldwide — one of WB's all-time highest-grossing films ever.
