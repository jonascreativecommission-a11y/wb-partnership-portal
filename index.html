The Founders

Harry Warner

CO-FOUNDER & PRESIDENT (1923–1956)

The eldest brother who ran the New York headquarters, overseeing finance and distribution. He championed the motto: 'Pictures that educate, enlighten, and entertain.'

Sam Warner

CHIEF TECHNOLOGY VISIONARY

The visionary who bet the studio on synchronized sound technology, leading to 'The Jazz Singer' (1927) and the birth of 'talkies'.

Jack L. Warner

HEAD OF PRODUCTION (1923–1967)

The youngest brother and Hollywood showman who signed legends like Humphrey Bogart and Bette Davis, leading the studio through its Golden Era.
